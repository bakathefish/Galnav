GalNav starlight + pulsar navigation demo How it works

Interstellar navigation, live

A photo of stars in, your position out.

Upload or pick a spacecraft star-field image. The tool plate-solves it, finds the nearby Gaia stars in the frame, and intersects the lines of sight to recover where the spacecraft is — in au, with an error ellipsoid. Same physics as the science spine; this is just a friendlier shell.

Frames

Preview

Select a frame to see its detected stars (cyan circles) and identified nearby stars (amber crosses).

detected = a blob the camera saw · identified = we know which catalogued star it is (its distance is shown) · position-capable = close enough that its parallax shift reveals where the spacecraft is. Most blobs are distant/faint field stars beyond the nearby catalog — only the amber ones can navigate.

Drag to orbit, scroll to zoom. Amber = your recovered position; the toggle at the top of the view switches to the 1,941-star nearby-stars scene. Spacecraft markers are approximate 2025-26 positions (cited).

How it works & honest limits. Five stages: plate-solve → centroid → age the catalog → identify nearby stars → intersect the lines of position.
  • One image gives a line of position, not a point — you need ≥2 different nearby stars.
  • A random photo of the sky usually has no star within 20 pc in frame, so it can't be fixed (pointing still works).
  • An uploaded image with no embedded WCS needs a blind solver (local astrometry.net via WSL, or a nova API key).
  • The ground-based frames fix the observer — they land on Earth. The demo (New Horizons) frames land at ~47 au.
