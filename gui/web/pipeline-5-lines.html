Step 5 of 6 — Lines of position

A direction alone is not a position. But a direction to a star whose position we know is a line of position: the spacecraft must lie on the line that runs from the star back along the measured sightline. Written out, the observer sits at

observer = s − λ û (λ > 0) s = the star's known catalog position (the anchor), û = the measured direction to it. One star pins a line; you need two lines from two different nearby stars to cross at a point.
