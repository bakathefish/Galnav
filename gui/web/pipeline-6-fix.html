Step 6 of 6 — The fix

With two or more lines from different stars, your position is where they cross. Real measurements are noisy, so the lines never meet perfectly; a least-squares intersection finds the point closest to all of them, and the curvature of that fit gives the 1-σ error ellipsoid. For the New Horizons demo the recovered point is compared to the JPL Horizons truth — the miss.
