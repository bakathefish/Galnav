Step 4 of 6 — From pixel to direction (the angles)

Each position-capable star's centroid is turned into a unit direction in space. The plate solution is a TAN (gnomonic tangent-plane) projection, so we de-project the pixel through its inverse to a sky coordinate, then to a 3-vector:

pixel (x, y) —WCS TAN⁻¹→ (RA α, Dec δ) → û = [ cosδ cosα, cosδ sinα, sinδ ] The gnomonic (tangent-plane) de-projection is exact for a TAN WCS; û is the measured spacecraft→star line of sight, a pure direction with no distance in it yet.
