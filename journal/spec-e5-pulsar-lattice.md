## Where this sits in the project

E1 and E6 are the STARLIGHT story: how well you navigate on star angles, and
how that decays as the catalog ages. E5-lite is the PULSAR story's first
result, and it is a negative one: it shows quantitatively why you cannot
switch to pulsar-comb navigation once you are between the stars. That "why the
obvious backup fails" result is what makes the starlight-fusion argument
honest — we are not ignoring pulsars, we are proving they do not close the gap
at interstellar range. It uses only PUBLIC pulsar facts (rotational periods),
so it needs neither the truth simulator nor the navigator solver.

It deliberately stops SHORT of the full pulsar navigator. The general
closest-vector integer-recovery solver (LAMBDA / LLL, optionally via `fpylll`)
is a real derivation-and-dependency fork, deferred to a follow-up card. E5-lite
needs only the packing radius, which is computed by bounded integer
enumeration in numpy.

## Prior art and the PRECISE claim (prior-art re-sweep 2026-07-16)

Be careful with the wording above ("why you cannot switch to pulsar-comb
navigation between the stars") — the re-sweep shows the honest claim is
NARROWER, and the paper must use the narrow version. What is ALREADY known:
pulsar navigation is an established SOLAR-SYSTEM technique ([Deng13] Earth-Mars
~20 km; Shemar 2018; SEXTANT/NICER); the pulse cycle/integer-ambiguity problem
is known and, in the solar system, resolvable with a crude prior (hypothesis
testing; Hou & Putnam); and at least one review claims pulsar nav works "and
beyond" the solar system ([Becker13]). So E5-lite does NOT discover that
pulsars fail interstellar navigation. Its DEFENSIBLE, specific contribution is:
in a FUSED starlight+pulsar system, a ~1 au starlight fix (the Bailer-Jones
regime, [BJ21]) is 4+ orders of magnitude too coarse to serve as the prior
needed to lock even the widest comb — so the star leg cannot BOOTSTRAP the
pulsar leg's integer ambiguity at interstellar range — quantified via the
lattice packing radius ([LAMBDA]). Frame the paper as that bootstrapping limit,
not as a blanket impossibility. (Full verdict + citations: logbook 2026-07-16
prior-art re-sweep; ratification item cc.)

## The physics, one symbol at a time

**Comb spacing.** A pulsar spins with period `P` (seconds). A photon
time-of-arrival measures its rotational PHASE, which is identical after every
full turn. So the measurement fixes the spacecraft's position along the
sightline `n_hat` only up to a whole number of turns. One turn of light travel
is the comb spacing

    s = c * P            [km]

- `c` = speed of light = `C_KM_S` = 299792.458 km/s (SI/IAU exact, from
  `galnav/units.py`).
- `P` = pulsar rotational period, seconds (ATNF catalogue, citation [ATNF]).
- `s` = comb spacing, km: the distance light covers in one period, i.e. the
  along-sightline position ambiguity. `comb_spacing_km(P)` returns exactly
  `C_KM_S * P` — nothing else.

**Coast time to lost lock.** Once an integer turn-count is locked, a velocity
error `sigma_v` drifts the position. The integer slips once the drift reaches
HALF a comb (the packing-radius window, below), so the coast time is

    T = (s / 2) / sigma_v            [seconds]  ->  /86400 -> [days]

- `sigma_v` = velocity uncertainty, km/s.
- `s/2` = half the comb spacing, km (the ± window around the locked integer).
- `coast_time_days(s, sigma_v)` returns `0.5 * s / sigma_v / 86400`.

**The ambiguity lattice.** With `K = 3` full-rank sightlines stacked as the
rows of `N` (3×3), and comb spacings `s = (s_1, s_2, s_3)`, the positions
consistent with all three phase measurements are

    r = N^{-1} ( phase + s ∘ m ),   m ∈ Z^3

so the set of position AMBIGUITIES (offsets from changing the integers) is the
lattice generated by

    B = N^{-1} diag(s)              [km]

- `m` = integer turn-count vector, one integer per pulsar.
- `∘` = elementwise product (each integer scaled by its own comb spacing).
- `B` = lattice generator, 3×3 km; its integer combinations `B m` are the
  position ambiguities. `ambiguity_lattice_generator(N, s)` returns `inv(N) @ diag(s)`.

**Shortest vector and packing radius.** The shortest nonzero lattice vector is

    lambda_1 = min over m ≠ 0 of || B m ||        [km]

and the PACKING RADIUS is

    rho = lambda_1 / 2             [km]

— the largest position uncertainty for which the correct integer is still
unambiguous on every comb. `shortest_vector_km(B)` enumerates
`m ∈ [-2, 2]^3` and takes the smallest nonzero `||B m||`; `packing_radius_km`
halves it.

**The impossibility.** A starlight position fix is `~1 au = AU_KM ≈ 1.5e8 km`.
The widest comb is the Crab at `s = 10,073 km`. The ratio

    (1 au in km) / (widest comb) = 1.5e8 / 1.0073e4 ≈ 1.5e4

is more than four orders of magnitude; against the finest comb (467 km) it is
`~3e5`. So the star-fix uncertainty dwarfs the packing radius of any comb set,
and no integer turn-count can be selected. That is the E5-lite headline.

## What the code does — and does NOT do

DOES: compute comb spacings `c*P`; compute the half-comb coast time; build the
3-pulsar ambiguity-lattice generator, its shortest vector (by bounded
enumeration), and packing radius; and (in the experiment) draw the comb-vs-
star-fix gap and the 467 km coast budget, saving every plotted array to an
`.npz` regenerable via `replot_from_npz`.

Does NOT: solve the general closest-vector problem (no LAMBDA/LLL, no `fpylll`);
handle `K ≠ 3` (v1 requires exactly three sightlines and raises otherwise); model
pulsar timing noise, glitches, dispersion, or the DM/relativistic corrections a
real photon-phase pipeline needs (that is Spec 9 / E4 armor with PINT); or claim
`lambda_1` is provably minimal for pathological, badly-conditioned geometries
(the radius-2 box is sufficient for the well-separated geometries used, and the
impossibility margin is 4+ orders regardless).

## Tolerances touched — and why each is exactly what it is

One golden WAS added — **authorized override #8: `COMB_MATCH_KM = 1.0`** (the
1 km comb-match gate that T2 uses, described just below). (Correction, 2026-07-16:
an earlier draft of this section wrongly read "No NEW tolerance was introduced;
`tests/golden_numbers.py` is untouched." That draft was written before the
override landed and was never updated; override #8 is real, is recorded in the
logbook, and gates T2. Value = 1.0, evidence in its golden comment.) Every other
oracle is either computed inline (exact) or read from the frozen golden file:

- **`COMB_KM` (frozen)** — the students' hand-derived §12 comb spacings. Test T2
  requires `c*P` to land within **1 km** of each. The 1 km is Spec 8's own
  published acceptance figure ("all six comb spacings match §12 to 1 km"), not
  an invented precision tolerance. Measured worst gap: J0030+0451 at 0.51 km.
  FLAG (ratification): J0030's frozen value 1459 km rounds UP from `c*P =
  1458.49 km` (which rounds to 1458); the discrepancy is 0.51 km, inside the
  1 km spec, but the frozen value is the "wrong" nearest integer. Recorded for a
  student ruling — the golden file is frozen, so this is flagged, not changed.
- **`COAST_DAYS_467KM_1CM_S = 270.0` and `COAST_DAYS_467KM_1M_S = 2.7` (frozen)**
  — test T3 rounds the computed coast time to each oracle's own stated precision
  (0 and 1 decimal places) and asks for equality: `round(270.25, 0) = 270.0`,
  `round(2.7025, 1) = 2.7`. Rounding to the oracle's precision needs no invented
  tolerance.
- **Exact (golden-free) oracles**: T1 asserts `comb_spacing_km(P) == C_KM_S * P`
  bitwise (`np.array_equal`); T3 asserts the coast formula and the exact 100×
  scaling; T5a asserts that orthonormal sightlines with equal combs `s0` give
  `lambda_1 == s0` exactly. These are zero-tolerance identities.
- **Scientific inequalities**: T4 and T5b assert strict `>` gaps
  (`1 au / widest comb > 1e4`, `1 au / rho > 1e4`). These are the finding
  itself, true by many orders of margin — not precision gates.

## Every test and what it would catch

`tests/test_e5_pulsar.py`, six tests:

- **T1 `test_comb_spacing_is_exactly_c_times_period`** — bitwise `c*P`. Catches
  any extra factor, unit slip, or wrong constant (an exact-equality gate cannot
  be fudged).
- **T2 `test_comb_spacings_match_frozen_section12_within_1km`** — the module's
  six combs reproduce the frozen §12 oracles to 1 km, and cover exactly those
  six pulsars. Catches a wrong period, a missing/extra pulsar, or a c/P swap.
- **T3 `test_coast_time_matches_frozen_467km_budgets`** — the 467 km coast
  budgets (9 months at 1 cm/s, 3 days at 1 m/s) and the exact half-comb formula
  and 100× scaling. Catches a full-comb-vs-half-comb error, a seconds/days slip,
  or a wrong power of `sigma_v`.
- **T4 `test_star_fix_cannot_lock_any_comb_four_order_gap`** — the 4-order gap to
  the widest comb and 5-order to the finest. Catches an au/km unit error or a
  sign of the argument being inverted; this is the headline as a gate.
- **T5a `test_orthonormal_lattice_shortest_vector_is_the_comb`** — exact lattice
  oracle: orthonormal sightlines, equal combs `s0` → `lambda_1 == s0`. Catches
  any error in `inv(N) @ diag(s)` or in the shortest-vector search.
- **T5b `test_packing_radius_is_km_scale_and_cannot_lock_a_star_fix`** — a real
  3-pulsar geometry gives a km-scale packing radius (bounded by the widest comb)
  that a 1 au fix beats by >1e4; and the packing radius is deterministic.
  Catches a lattice that collapses (rho → 0) or explodes past the combs, and any
  nondeterminism the experiment would inherit.
